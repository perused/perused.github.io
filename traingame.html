Train Game

What is the Train Game?

In short, you need to add four numbers together using only plus, minus, multiply, divide and brackets, to equal 10 (rules can vary). For an extended explanation on the origins of this game, read below.

The Train Game seems to be a rite of passage for young Sydney-siders, who are taught the game by a friend around the time that they start going out without their parents to movies and whatnot. Every train carriage in Sydney has a four digit number identifying the carriage. Upon stepping upon the carriage with friends, without acknowledging, all friends unanimously begin to stare at the train numbers until they have figured out how they can get it to add to 10. Almost inevitably, someone will have used an invalid operator, like 'the power of' or 'floor division', and an argument will ensue. It makes for a very entertaining way of passing the time on the train.

In my game above, I require that the four numbers must be solved 'in order', but this does not mean that one cannot use brackets to somewhat change the order of operations.

I created this program in order to validate or 'cheat' on the train game (cheat only if you already tried though!).
